Authentic Nigerian Afang Soup Recipe

Afang soup is a rich, flavorful vegetable soup from Southern Nigeria, loved for its unique blend of fresh waterleaf and finely sliced okazi leaves. Slow-cooked with tender meat, stockfish, and a mix of traditional spices, it delivers a hearty, earthy taste that pairs perfectly with fufu, pounded yam, or eba. Popular among the Efik and Ibibio people, this nourishing dish is a celebration of culture and comfort in every bite.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Number of servings: 6

Difficulty Level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 500g beef and assorted meat (cut into chunks)
  • 200g stockfish (pre-soaked)
  • 2 cups finely sliced okazi (afang) leaves
  • ½ cup ground crayfish
  • ½ cup palm oil
  • 2 cups chopped waterleaf
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Add Salt to taste
  • 1 liter water (adjust as needed)

Instructions

  1. Wash and season the beef and assorted meat with salt, seasoning cubes, and a little pepper, then cook until tender.
  2. Add the stockfish to the pot and continue cooking until soft and well incorporated with the beef stock.
  3. Stir in the palm oil, ground crayfish, and remaining pepper. Allow to simmer for 5 minutes so the flavors blend.
  4. Add the chopped waterleaf and cook for about 3–4 minutes until slightly wilted.
  5. Mix in the finely sliced okazi (afang) leaves, adjust seasoning with salt if needed, and cook for another 3–5 minutes. Remove from heat and serve hot with fufu, pounded yam, or eba.

Tips

  • Soak the stockfish in warm water for at least 30 minutes to soften it before cooking.
  • Wash the waterleaf thoroughly to remove sand and grit.
  • Slice okazi leaves thinly or grind so they blend well into the soup.

Nutrition Facts (per serving)

Afang soup is packed with protein from the beef and stockfish, healthy fats from palm oil, and fiber from the vegetables. While it’s nutrient-rich and filling, portion control is key if you’re watching calorie intake.
